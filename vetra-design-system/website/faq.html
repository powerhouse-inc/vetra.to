FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often.

What is Vetra and who is it for?

−
Vetra is a design infrastructure platform built for product teams. It helps designers and developers collaborate seamlessly by providing a shared source of truth for design tokens, components, and documentation. Whether you're a startup or an enterprise, Vetra scales with your needs.

How does pricing work?

+
We offer a free tier for small teams and individual designers. Paid plans start at $12/user/month and include advanced features like version history, branching, and custom integrations. Enterprise plans with SSO and dedicated support are available on request.

Can I import my existing design tokens?

−
Absolutely. Vetra supports importing tokens from JSON, YAML, Style Dictionary, and Figma. Our migration wizard walks you through the process step by step, and typically takes less than 10 minutes for most design systems.

Does Vetra integrate with Figma?

+
Yes, we have a native Figma plugin that syncs your design tokens bi-directionally. Changes made in Figma are reflected in your codebase and vice versa, keeping your design and development teams perfectly aligned.

What frameworks and platforms do you support?

−
Vetra generates code for React, Vue, Svelte, Angular, iOS (Swift), and Android (Kotlin). We also provide CSS custom properties, SCSS variables, and Tailwind config outputs. If your platform isn't listed, our API allows you to build custom transformers.

Is there a free trial?

+
Yes. All paid plans come with a 14-day free trial, no credit card required. You get full access to all features during the trial period so you can evaluate Vetra with your actual projects and workflows.

How secure is my data?

+
Security is our top priority. All data is encrypted at rest and in transit using AES-256 and TLS 1.3. We're SOC 2 Type II certified, conduct annual penetration tests, and offer SSO with SAML 2.0 on enterprise plans.

Can I use Vetra with my existing CI/CD pipeline?

−
Yes. Vetra provides a CLI tool and GitHub Action that integrates directly into your CI/CD workflow. You can automate token publishing, run visual regression checks, and enforce design consistency as part of your pull request process.
